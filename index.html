How I work

1

One conversation before anything else. I understand your business, what you sell, who buys it, and what's not working. No proposals before this.

A few things
people ask.

If something isn't answered here, the easiest thing is to just reach out directly.

Usually it's not traffic — it's clarity. Visitors arrive but can't quickly understand why your product is right for them, or something in the experience creates hesitation before checkout. The design can look fine while the structure underneath quietly breaks the sale. That's exactly what a store redesign is meant to find and fix.

Store redesigns usually take 2–4 weeks. New store setups typically take 1–2 weeks. The timeline depends on scope, and I'll always be clear about it before we start — not after.

Not exclusively. But I work best with brands that have already proven demand — meaning customers want what you're selling, even if the system around it needs work. If you're still figuring out product-market fit, a store redesign won't solve that yet.

Direct, fast communication — you hear from me, not someone passing messages along. I ask questions when I need to, share progress at each stage, and don't disappear between calls. Three revision rounds are included in every project, and I'm clear about scope before we begin.

Pricing depends on what the project actually needs — scope, complexity, and your stage as a business. I'd rather give you a clear, honest number after a short conversation than post a rate that either over-promises or undersells. Reach out and we'll figure it out together.

On-page fundamentals — clean URL structure, meta setup, page speed — are part of every redesign. A full discoverability foundation (keyword research, content structure, analytics) is a separate service. I'll recommend it if your stage genuinely calls for it, and not otherwise.
